📖 Project Overview

This project examines the digital "re-branding" of Gibran Rakabuming Raka, whose political persona has been steadily polished for wider public consumption. Once known for his awkward, sometimes quirky communication style, Gibran now presents himself as a more formal and serious figure on YouTube. Videos such as “Generasi Muda, Demographic Bonus, and The Future of Indonesia” suggest a deliberate effort to appear statesmanlike — an image not always consistent with his earlier public appearances.

📊 Sentiment Analysis: Polarity & Subjectivity Scatter Plot

Polarity (X-axis): -1 = very negative, +1 = very positive.
Subjectivity (Y-axis): 0 = objective/factual, 1 = subjective/opinion.

Key Findings

  • Most comments fall in the subjective area (>0.5), indicating opinion-based discussions.
  • Red dots (negative) dominate left side; blue dots (positive) cluster on the right.
  • Overall tone leans toward neutral–negative polarity with high subjectivity.

💡 Overall Insights

  • The discourse is opinion-driven rather than fact-based.
  • Negative comments are diverse and scattered.
  • Positive comments are fewer but more consistent in tone.
